Certificate Course in Child and Adolescent Counselling

Specialized focus on age-specific psychological needs and counselling techniques for children and adolescents, with emphasis on developmental milestones, emotional challenges, and behavioural management strategies.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Developmental psychology across childhood and adolescence
  • Common childhood and teenage behavioral issues
  • School-based counselling techniques and interventions
  • Parent and teacher collaboration strategies
  • Play therapy and creative interventions
  • Adolescent identity development and challenges
  • Bullying prevention and intervention

Learning Outcomes:

  • Gain insight into emotional and behavioral development
  • Apply age-appropriate counselling techniques
  • Address academic, social, and emotional challenges effectively
  • Work collaboratively with parents, schools, and caregivers
  • Implement developmentally appropriate interventions
Duration: 5-6 Weeks

Certificate Course in Neurodevelopmental Disorders and Behavioural Management

Comprehensive understanding of major neurodevelopmental disorders such as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), ADHD, and Intellectual Disability, along with evidence-based behavior management techniques and intervention strategies.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Identification and assessment of neurodevelopmental disorders
  • Early intervention and behavioral therapy techniques
  • Classroom strategies and environmental modifications
  • Family support and parent training programs
  • Individualized Education Plans (IEPs) development
  • Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) principles
  • Sensory processing and integration strategies
  • Transition planning for adolescents and young adults

Learning Outcomes:

  • Recognize and differentiate among common neurodevelopmental conditions
  • Implement effective behavior management strategies
  • Design supportive intervention plans for diverse settings
  • Collaborate with multidisciplinary teams for holistic care
  • Support families in navigating service systems
Duration: 3-4 Weeks

Certificate Course in Essential Counselling Skills

Build foundational counselling competencies needed for effective therapeutic communication, including active listening, empathy, rapport-building, and ethical practice. Perfect for beginners and those seeking to strengthen their counselling foundation.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Core conditions of counselling (Rogers' approach)
  • Advanced verbal and non-verbal communication skills
  • Structured counselling sessions and techniques
  • Ethics, boundaries, and professional standards
  • Active listening and empathetic responding
  • Goal setting and treatment planning
  • Documentation and record keeping
  • Self-care and preventing burnout

Learning Outcomes:

  • Demonstrate essential interpersonal and counselling skills
  • Build therapeutic alliances across diverse populations
  • Structure and guide counselling sessions effectively
  • Practice with awareness of professional ethics and self-care
  • Apply person-centered counselling principles
Duration: 4-5 Weeks

Certificate Course in Psychological Architecture of Cancer Care (CPACC)

Unique program exploring the psychological dimensions of cancer care, addressing the complex mental, emotional, and existential concerns faced by cancer patients and their families throughout the cancer journey.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Psychological impact of cancer diagnosis and treatment phases
  • Grief, loss, anticipatory grief, and end-of-life care
  • Psycho-oncology frameworks and evidence-based interventions
  • Counsellor's role in palliative and terminal care settings
  • Family dynamics and caregiver support
  • Existential and spiritual concerns in cancer care
  • Communication skills for difficult conversations
  • Managing anxiety, depression, and trauma in cancer patients

Learning Outcomes:

  • Understand psychological stages of the cancer journey
  • Support cancer patients and caregivers through therapeutic interventions
  • Integrate psychological care in oncology settings
  • Communicate with compassion in high-stress healthcare environments
  • Address complex grief and existential concerns
